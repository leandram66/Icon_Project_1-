9/11 and it's Long Lasting Impact on Members of the FDNY

Leandra Manon

Sept. 30, 2023

The 9/11 Walk of Remembrance Memorial, held on September 10th, 2023, invited members of the E1/L24 FDNY alongside family and friends to honor the lives of Father Mychal Judge and Detective Steven McDonald lost during the attacks, as well as the many lives affected by the tragic events that took place 22 years ago.

“It’s a needed event and should never be forgotten,” said Irene Venditti.

Venditti and her longtime friend, Freyda Markow, attended the memorial in support of their loved ones lost during the attacks.

“For me, it's healing,” Venditti said in regards to the memorial service. “That's how I heal. But a week before, like her,” Venditti motioned to Markow, “we're already beginning the journey.”

Markow shakes her head in agreement. “It never ends," laments Markow." I mean, there are days during the course of the year where 9/11 will hit us because we’re still losing so many of our friends and family.”

Markow’s sentiments ring true. Members of the FDNY who survived the attacks are continuing to feel the effects of 9/11 emotionally and physically years later.

A study published back in 2015 depicts that numerous FDNY members were exposed to harmful chemicals and debris on the day of the attacks. As a result, many are facing long term effects due to the exposure.

According to the Never Forget Project, nearly 14 years after the tragic events, over 7,000 FDNY firefighters and EMTs have received treatment for a 9/11 related injury or illness.

Health Problems facing 9/11 Responders and Survivors:

Below are a few of the illnesses that have impacted the lives of those exposed during the attacks.

Upper Respiratory

5,200 members have been diagnosed with upper respiratory diseases such as chronic rhinosinusitis and/or vocal cord diseases.

Mental Health

3,700 members have been diagnosed with mental health stress-related conditions, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) being the most common mental health disorder.

Gastroesophageal Reflux

5,400 members have been diagnosed with gastroesophageal reflux disorders.

Cancer

1,100 have developed a cancer caused by 9/11 toxins. Of those, 44 have died despite access to treatment.

Data source: http://neverforgetproject.com/statistics
